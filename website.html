Understanding Frequency

Frequency is the cycles per second of a medium.

Frequency is defined as, "the rate at which a vibration occurs that constitutes a wave, either in a material (as in sound waves), or in an electromagnetic field (as in radio waves and light), usually measured per second."

'Frequency' and 'vibration' are interchangeable terms.

So what is vibration?



Understanding Vibration

Vibration is the pulsing of a medium.

For example, the pulsing of air is what we call 'sound.'

Vibration is defined as, "an oscillation of the parts of a fluid or an elastic solid whose equilibrium has been disturbed, or of an electromagnetic wave."

Although frequency and vibration are interchangeable, they are, however, used to identify different - energetic phenomena.



Understanding Light

Light is what enables vision.

Matter is trapped light.

'Light' is what we call 'frequency.'

However, frequency is also used to substantiate sound, so what is sound?



Understanding Sound

Sound is what enables hearing.

Geometry is solidifed sound, and geometry is the form--the matter.

'Sound' is defined as, "vibrations that travel through the air or another medium and can be heard when they reach a person's or animal's ear."

Frequency is light, vibration is sound, and matter is form.
